# Brand & Voice Context

This file governs every word of user-facing copy on the site.
The portfolio's credibility lives or dies on its voice. Read
this before writing any copy.

## Who Is Speaking

This is **Afeez's** voice — first person, founder of Refacint
Technologies. The agency site (refacint.com) speaks as the
company ("We build..."). This site speaks as the person ("I
build..."). Keep that distinction consistent.

## Voice

- **Confident, straight-talking, human.** Founder-led, not
  corporate-agency. Punchy over formal.
- **Specific over generic.** Concrete details, real examples,
  named tools and outcomes beat vague positioning.
- **Minimal jargon.** Explain what things do in plain terms. A
  Lagos SMB owner and a global prospect should both follow it.
- **Locally grounded, globally capable.** Lagos/Nigerian market
  knowledge and a real delivery track record are the genuine
  moat — lead with that, not with tool access.
- First person throughout the founder's own sections (Hero,
  About, Consulting, Contact).

## Hard Rules

These are non-negotiable. They come from real corrections made
during the agency build.

1. **No fabricated claims or invented stories.** Never invent
   client anecdotes, metrics, testimonials, outcomes, awards,
   talks, or biographical facts. Every claim must be grounded
   in something verifiable. If a fact is missing, leave a
   clearly marked `TODO(content)` placeholder for the founder
   to fill — never plug the gap with plausible invention.
2. **No AI slop or generic phrasing.** Avoid hype language,
   filler, and repetitive framing. No "cutting-edge",
   "leverage synergies", "in today's fast-paced world",
   "unlock your potential", "game-changing", "passionate
   about", or similar. If a sentence could appear on any
   agency's site, rewrite it until it could only appear on
   this one.
3. **No fixed pricing / no dollar figures.** Consulting and
   service copy uses flexible payment-terms language suited to
   the Nigerian market. Do not state specific prices in any
   currency. Talk about engagement shape and value, not
   numbers.
4. **No "30-day" / overnight-success framing.** The "30 days
   to replace your income" trope common in AI-monetization
   content is marketing shorthand and is off-brand. Realistic
   timelines (when relevant) are 6-12 months. Be honest about
   what takes time.
5. **No fake urgency or manipulative CTAs.** CTAs are direct
   and respectful ("Work with me", "Book a call", "Invite me
   to speak"), not "Act now — limited spots!".

## What Good Copy Looks Like

- Sentences a real founder would say out loud.
- Claims a skeptical prospect could verify.
- Concrete nouns and verbs over adjectives.
- Short. Cut every word that is not doing work (Kevin's rule:
  "Why waste time say lot word when few word do trick?").
- Specific proof: real clients (MOLEK Schools, Klassrun
  Technologies, Tenderville School), real problems solved, real
  approaches.

## Anti-AI-Slop Checklist (Humanizer)

Every piece of user-facing copy — section text, project
descriptions, the About, FAQ answers, and especially blog posts —
must pass this checklist before it ships. These are the
machine-writing tells that make copy read as generic AI output.
Avoid them when writing; hunt them down when editing. (For
important prose, Afeez also runs a separate humanizing pass — but
the goal is to not generate the slop in the first place.)

**Significance inflation — cut it.** No "stands as / serves as a
testament / a pivotal moment / marks a shift / reflects a broader
/ evolving landscape / key turning point / leaves an indelible
mark / setting the stage for." Don't puff up importance; state
the thing.

**Promotional tone — cut it.** No "vibrant, rich, profound,
groundbreaking, renowned, breathtaking, must-visit, stunning,
nestled, in the heart of, boasts a, commitment to." This is the
single biggest risk for an agency/founder site — write plainly,
let the work speak.

**Superficial "-ing" tails — cut them.** No clauses bolted on the
end like "…, highlighting its importance," "…, ensuring
success," "…, reflecting a deep connection," "…, showcasing." They
fake depth and add nothing.

**Vague attributions — cut them.** No "industry observers note,
experts argue, studies show, some critics say" without a real,
named source. If there's no real source, drop the claim.

**Overused AI vocabulary — avoid.** delve, leverage (as filler),
crucial, pivotal, enhance, foster, underscore, highlight (verb),
intricate, interplay, tapestry, landscape (abstract), testament,
seamless, robust, vibrant, vital, garner, align with,
additionally. If a word feels like LLM default, replace it with a
plain one.

**Use "is/are" — stop avoiding the copula.** Prefer "Refacint is
a Lagos agency" over "Refacint serves as / stands as / represents
a Lagos agency."

**Negative parallelism — cut it.** No "It's not just X, it's Y,"
"Not only… but also," and no clipped tailing negations ("…, no
guessing," "…, no wasted motion"). Write the real clause.

**Rule of three — break it.** Don't force everything into tidy
triplets ("faster, smarter, better"; "talks, panels, networking").
Vary the count; two or four is fine.

**Synonym cycling — stop.** Don't rename the same thing every
sentence (the founder / the builder / the operator / he). Repeat
the plain word.

**False ranges — cut.** No "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't a
real scale ("from startups to enterprises, from idea to impact").

**Em-dash overuse — cut.** Prefer commas, periods, or
parentheses. An occasional em dash is fine; a paragraph full of
them is a tell.

**Boldface & emoji — minimal.** No mechanical bolding of phrases,
no inline-header bullet lists ("**Speed:** …, **Quality:** …"),
no emoji decoration. Headings in sentence case, not Title Case.

**Straight quotes**, not curly. **Don't over-hyphenate** common
pairs (client-facing, data-driven, high-quality, end-to-end,
real-time) — humans don't hyphenate these uniformly.

**No chatbot artifacts** — "Great question," "I hope this helps,"
"Let me know if," "Of course!" Never paste assistant-speak into
content.

**No hedging or knowledge-cutoff disclaimers** — "it could
potentially be argued," "while details are limited." Say it or
cut it.

**No generic upbeat conclusions** — "the future looks bright,"
"exciting times ahead," "a step in the right direction." End on
something concrete and real.

**No persuasive-authority tropes** — "the real question is," "at
its core," "what really matters," "fundamentally." They dress up
an ordinary point as deep truth.

**No fragmented headers** — a heading followed by a one-line
restatement of the heading. Get to the content.

**Have a pulse.** Clean isn't enough — soulless is also a tell.
Vary sentence rhythm (short, then longer). Use "I" where it fits
(this is a founder site). Have an opinion. Be specific about
detail and feeling. Let a little human mess in. Read it aloud — if
it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

## When Writing Copy

- Draft in the founder's voice, then cut.
- Flag any spot where you lack a real fact — propose a
  `TODO(content)` rather than filling it.
- Surface assumptions to the founder before shipping copy that
  makes a claim about him, the agency, or a client.
- When the founder edits copy to remove hype or correct a
  claim, treat that as a signal and apply the same standard
  everywhere else.
