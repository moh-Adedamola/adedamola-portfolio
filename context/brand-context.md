# Brand & Voice Context

This file governs every word of user-facing copy on the site.
The portfolio's credibility lives or dies on its voice. Read
this before writing any copy.

## Who Is Speaking

This is **Afeez's** voice — first person, founder of Refacint
Technologies. The agency site (refacint.com) speaks as the
company ("We build..."). This site speaks as the person ("I
build..."). Keep that distinction consistent.

## Voice

- **Confident, straight-talking, human.** Founder-led, not
  corporate-agency. Punchy over formal.
- **Specific over generic.** Concrete details, real examples,
  named tools and outcomes beat vague positioning.
- **Minimal jargon.** Explain what things do in plain terms. A
  Lagos SMB owner and a global prospect should both follow it.
- **Locally grounded, globally capable.** Lagos/Nigerian market
  knowledge and a real delivery track record are the genuine
  moat — lead with that, not with tool access.
- First person throughout the founder's own sections (Hero,
  About, Consulting, Contact).

## Hard Rules

These are non-negotiable. They come from real corrections made
during the agency build.

1. **No fabricated claims or invented stories.** Never invent
   client anecdotes, metrics, testimonials, outcomes, awards,
   talks, or biographical facts. Every claim must be grounded
   in something verifiable. If a fact is missing, leave a
   clearly marked `TODO(content)` placeholder for the founder
   to fill — never plug the gap with plausible invention.
2. **No AI slop or generic phrasing.** Avoid hype language,
   filler, and repetitive framing. No "cutting-edge",
   "leverage synergies", "in today's fast-paced world",
   "unlock your potential", "game-changing", "passionate
   about", or similar. If a sentence could appear on any
   agency's site, rewrite it until it could only appear on
   this one.
3. **No fixed pricing / no dollar figures.** Consulting and
   service copy uses flexible payment-terms language suited to
   the Nigerian market. Do not state specific prices in any
   currency. Talk about engagement shape and value, not
   numbers.
4. **No "30-day" / overnight-success framing.** The "30 days
   to replace your income" trope common in AI-monetization
   content is marketing shorthand and is off-brand. Realistic
   timelines (when relevant) are 6-12 months. Be honest about
   what takes time.
5. **No fake urgency or manipulative CTAs.** CTAs are direct
   and respectful ("Work with me", "Book a call", "Invite me
   to speak"), not "Act now — limited spots!".

## What Good Copy Looks Like

- Sentences a real founder would say out loud.
- Claims a skeptical prospect could verify.
- Concrete nouns and verbs over adjectives.
- Short. Cut every word that is not doing work (Kevin's rule:
  "Why waste time say lot word when few word do trick?").
- Specific proof: real clients (MOLEK Schools, Klassrun
  Technologies, Tenderville School), real problems solved, real
  approaches.

## Design References and Voice

Two reference sites guide the build (`design-reference.md`):
Vysta (growwithvysta.com) for visual polish, Stanlee
(stanlee.vercel.app) for content. Critical voice note: borrow
Vysta's **structure and confidence**, never its **hype**. Its
inflated metrics, absolutist claims, manufactured urgency, and
"30-day" money framing all violate the hard rules above.
Stanlee's honest, first-person, story-driven voice is the
model to emulate — including its candor. Confident and
polished on the outside; honest and specific on the inside.

## The Blog Voice

The portfolio hosts the founder's **own** blog (separate from

on AI/automation in the Lagos/Nigerian market, and the kind of

("we", service/SEO content); the portfolio blog is "I". Keep
the two distinct — the same hard rules apply (real, specific,
no slop, no fabricated claims, no fixed-price or "30-day"
framing). Honest, founder-led, worth reading.

## When Writing Copy

- Draft in the founder's voice, then cut.
- Flag any spot where you lack a real fact — propose a
  `TODO(content)` rather than filling it.
- Surface assumptions to the founder before shipping copy that
  makes a claim about him, the agency, or a client.
- When the founder edits copy to remove hype or correct a
  claim, treat that as a signal and apply the same standard
  everywhere else.
